# The Lao AI Quality Problem

This document describes common quality issues that appear in Lao-language AI, OCR, parser, and generated-text workflows. It is not an attack on any person, organization, product, or project.

## Common Issues

### Thai-Influenced Structure

Lao output may follow Thai word order, phrasing, or punctuation habits instead of natural Lao usage. This can make text feel close but not fully Lao-native.

### Translation-Like Lao

Some generated Lao reads like a direct translation from English, Thai, or another language. It may be understandable but awkward, overly formal, or unnatural.

### Weak Local Context

AI systems may miss local names, administrative terms, educational context, cultural context, market usage, or everyday Lao phrasing.

### Inconsistent Spelling and Wording

Lao spelling and spacing can vary across sources. AI systems may mix forms without explaining whether the variation is acceptable, outdated, domain-specific, or simply wrong.

### Missing Source Authority

Generated local facts are often presented without clear sources. For Lao-language work, a fluent answer is not enough. Source authority and review status matter.

### Hallucinated Local Facts

AI systems may invent Lao local facts, organization names, place details, statistics, or policy claims. These errors can look confident and polished.

### OCR Errors

Lao OCR can confuse letters, tone marks, vowels, spacing, line breaks, and mixed-script text. OCR output should be treated as candidate text until reviewed.

### Overconfidence From Models

AI systems often answer with high confidence even when the input is incomplete, the source is weak, or the Lao output has not been checked by a native speaker.

## LAKI Response

LAKI responds by emphasizing source authority, human review, explicit uncertainty, small reviewed examples, OCR evaluation, parser validation, and public-safe methodology.
