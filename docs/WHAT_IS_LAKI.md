# What Is LAKI?

LAKI stands for Lao AI Knowledge Infrastructure.

LAKI is infrastructure, not just a chatbot. It is a public project profile and methodology space for improving the foundations that Lao AI systems depend on: data quality, source checking, human review, OCR evaluation, parser logic, benchmarks, and safe knowledge workflows.

## Infrastructure, Not Only Output

Many AI projects are judged by the final answer a model gives. LAKI focuses earlier in the pipeline:

- What source did the information come from?
- Is the Lao wording natural and locally appropriate?
- Was the text produced by OCR, a parser, a human, or an AI model?
- What review status does the item have?
- Can the result be traced, corrected, or rejected?
- Is it safe to publish?

## What LAKI Can Support

LAKI methodology can support:

- AI tools that need better Lao-language data quality
- language models that need safer Lao evaluation examples
- OCR systems that need Lao error analysis
- parsers that need reviewed input/output examples
- research datasets that need provenance and status fields
- local applications that need source-aware Lao knowledge
- benchmarks that separate fluent text from verified information

## Why This Matters

Good Lao AI requires more than text generation. It needs careful handling of source authority, review status, uncertainty, spelling, wording, OCR mistakes, and local context.

LAKI treats Lao language data as something that deserves review and stewardship, not as disposable model output.
